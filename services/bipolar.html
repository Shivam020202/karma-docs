Understanding the Condition

More Than Just
Mood Swings

Bipolar Disorder is characterized by significant shifts in mood, energy, and activity levels. These aren't just normal ups and downs; they can be severe enough to affect relationships, job performance, and daily life.

At Karma Doctors & Associates, we understand clarity and stability are the goals. We offer a combination of medication management and therapy to help you navigate specific challenges and prevent relapse.

The Bipolar Spectrum

There are different types of Bipolar Disorder, each with unique patterns.

Bipolar I

Constructed by manic episodes that last at least 7 days or are severe enough to require hospital care. Depressive episodes typically occur as well.

  • Manic Episodes
  • Depressive Lows
Get Help

Bipolar II

A pattern of depressive episodes and hypomanic episodes (less severe than classic mania), but not the full-blown manic episodes of Bipolar I.

  • Hypomania
  • Major Depression
Get Help

Cyclothymia

Defined by numerous periods of hypomanic symptoms and numerous periods of depressive symptoms lasting for at least 2 years.

  • Chronic fluctuations
  • Consistent instability
Get Help
Warning Signs

Identifying
The Shifts

The key to management is recognizing the signals of an episode before it escalates.

Get Evaluated

Mania/Hypomania

Euphoria, sleeplessness, racing thoughts

Depression

Hopelessness, fatigue, loss of interest

Risk Taking

Impulsive spending, reckless behavior

Irritability

Extreme agitation or jumpiness

Sleep Changes

Insomnia (depression) or no need for sleep (mania)

Racing Thoughts

Fast, uncontrollable logic jumps
