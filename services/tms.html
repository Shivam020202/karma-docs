Innovative Care

Hope for
Treatment-Resistant Depression

If you've tried multiple antidepressants without success, you're not alone. TMS (Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation) is a breakthrough therapy that targets the root cause of depression in the brain using magnetic pulses.

It works by stimulating underactive areas of the brain that regulate mood. Unlike medication, TMS is non-systemic, meaning it doesn't circulate in the bloodstream and typically has few to no side effects.

How TMS Works

A typical course of treatment involves daily sessions over 4-6 weeks.

Comfortable Setting

You'll relax in a comfortable chair. No sedation is used, so you remain awake and alert throughout the 20-30 minute session.

Magnetic Pulses

A magnetic coil is placed gently against your head, delivering focused magnetic pulses to stimulate nerve cells in the brain.

Back to Your Day

Since there's no anesthesia, you can drive yourself home or back to work immediately after treatment.

Common Questions

Does TMS hurt?

Most patients find TMS well-tolerated. You may feel a tapping sensation on your scalp during treatment. Mild scalp discomfort or headache can occur initially but typically resolves quickly.

Is TMS covered by insurance?

Yes, most major insurance plans cover TMS for patients who have been diagnosed with Major Depressive Disorder and have not found relief from antidepressant medications.

How soon will I see results?

Response varies by patient. Some notice improvements within 2-3 weeks, while for others, the effect builds over the course of the 6-week treatment plan.
